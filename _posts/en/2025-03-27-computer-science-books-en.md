Here’s a curated list of 30 highly regarded computer science books spanning systems, programming, algorithms, networking, theory, and more, all comparable in depth and quality to *Computer Systems: A Programmer’s Perspective*:

### **Systems & Architecture**
1. **Computer Systems: A Programmer’s Perspective** by Bryant & O’Hallaron *(the inspiration for this list)*  
2. **Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces** by Remzi Arpaci-Dusseau  
3. **Computer Organization and Design (RISC-V Edition)** by Patterson & Hennessy  
4. **Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach** by Hennessy & Patterson  
5. **The Linux Programming Interface** by Michael Kerrisk  

---

### **Programming & Software Engineering**
6. **Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)** by Abelson, Sussman, & Sussman  
7. **The C Programming Language** by Kernighan & Ritchie  
8. **Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship** by Robert C. Martin  
9. **Code Complete** by Steve McConnell  
10. **Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software** by Gamma et al.  
11. **The Pragmatic Programmer** by Andrew Hunt & David Thomas  
12. **Refactoring: Improving the Design of Existing Code** by Martin Fowler  
13. **Programming Pearls** by Jon Bentley  

---

### **Algorithms & Theory**
14. **Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)** by Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, & Stein  
15. **Algorithms (4th Edition)** by Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne  
16. **The Art of Computer Programming** by Donald Knuth *(multi-volume classic)*  
17. **Introduction to the Theory of Computation** by Michael Sipser  
18. **Types and Programming Languages** by Benjamin C. Pierce  

---

### **Networking & Distributed Systems**
19. **Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach** by Kurose & Ross  
20. **Computer Networks** by Andrew Tanenbaum & David Wetherall  
21. **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** by Martin Kleppmann  

---

### **Compilers & Languages**
22. **Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools (Dragon Book)** by Aho et al.  
23. **Crafting Interpreters** by Robert Nystrom *(free online/print)*  
24. **Advanced Programming in the UNIX Environment** by W. Richard Stevens  

---

### **Security**
25. **Security Engineering** by Ross Anderson  
26. **Hacking: The Art of Exploitation** by Jon Erickson  

---

### **Databases**
27. **Database System Concepts** by Silberschatz, Korth, & Sudarshan  

---

### **Concurrency & Parallelism**
28. **Java Concurrency in Practice** by Brian Goetz et al.  

---

### **Specialized Topics**
29. **Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach** by Stuart Russell & Peter Norvig  
30. **Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid** by Douglas Hofstadter *(CS/math philosophy)*  

---

### **Bonus Classics**
- **The Mythical Man-Month** by Frederick Brooks *(software project management)*  
- **Code: The Hidden Language of Computer Hardware and Software** by Charles Petzold  
- **Cracking the Coding Interview** by Gayle Laakmann McDowell *(practical prep)*  

These books cover foundational concepts and modern practices, making them timeless resources for students, engineers, and enthusiasts. Let me know if you’d like deeper dives into specific topics!
