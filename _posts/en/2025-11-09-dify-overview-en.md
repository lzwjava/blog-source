### What is Dify.ai?
Dify.ai is an open-source, low-code platform for building and deploying generative AI applications powered by large language models (LLMs). Launched in 2023, it focuses on agentic workflows, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipelines, and AI agents, making it easier for developers, teams, and enterprises to create chatbots, copilots, and complex automations without deep coding. It's self-hostable, with a drag-and-drop interface, and emphasizes production-readiness through scalable infrastructure, model management, and a plugin marketplace. Think of it as a "LangChain in the cloud" but more user-friendly and visual, competing with tools like Flowise or Vercel AI.

As of November 2025, Dify is thriving in the AI agent boom, with strong community momentum and enterprise adoption. It's particularly popular in Asia (e.g., integrations with Alibaba Cloud) and for B2B use cases like customer support automation and knowledge pipelines.

### Key Features
- **Visual Workflow Builder**: Drag-and-drop for multi-step AI agents, including iteration loops, conditional logic, and tool orchestration.
- **RAG and Knowledge Pipelines**: Ingest data from web, databases, or files; index into vector stores; and enable context-aware responses.
- **LLM Flexibility**: Switch between 100+ models (open-source like Llama or proprietary like GPT/Claude) with built-in performance benchmarking.
- **Plugins and Integrations**: 120+ in the Marketplace, including recent 2025 additions like Firecrawl (web scraping), Qdrant/TiDB (vector storage), Tavily (search), Bright Data (structured data), TrueFoundry (AI gateway), and Arize (observability). Supports MCP for API/database bridging.
- **Deployment Options**: Cloud-hosted (Backend-as-a-Service), self-hosted, or embeddable; enterprise features like SSO, audit logs, and scalability for high traffic.
- **No-Code to Pro**: Beginner-friendly templates, but extensible with custom code, debugging tools, and monitoring.

Recent updates include v1.0.0 (Feb 2025) with decoupled plugins for easier extensibility, HTTP-based MCP support (Mar 2025 protocol), and a new official forum for community Q&A.
