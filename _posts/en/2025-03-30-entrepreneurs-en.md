Here is a list of **impactful entrepreneurs** who have made significant contributions to business, technology, and global industries:

### 1. **Steve Jobs (1955–2011)**
   - Co-founder of **Apple Inc.**, Jobs revolutionized personal computing, smartphones, and entertainment through innovations like the **iPhone**, **MacBook**, **iPod**, and **iTunes**.
   - His visionary leadership and focus on design and user experience set Apple apart as one of the most valuable companies in the world.

### 2. **Elon Musk (1971–Present)**
   - Founder of **SpaceX**, **Tesla**, **Neuralink**, and **The Boring Company**, Musk is known for his ambitious goals of **revolutionizing transportation**, **space exploration**, and **sustainable energy**.
   - Tesla has made electric vehicles mainstream, and SpaceX has reduced the cost of space travel, aiming for Mars colonization.

### 3. **Jeff Bezos (1964–Present)**
   - Founder of **Amazon**, Bezos transformed the way people shop online and revolutionized e-commerce, cloud computing (with **AWS**), and entertainment (through **Prime Video**).
   - His influence extends to the space industry with his venture **Blue Origin**, aiming to reduce the cost of space travel and make space exploration more accessible.

### 4. **Bill Gates (1955–Present)**
   - Co-founder of **Microsoft**, Gates played a pivotal role in making personal computers ubiquitous through the development of the **Windows** operating system.
   - Gates has also become a major philanthropist through the **Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation**, focusing on global health and education.

### 5. **Warren Buffett (1930–Present)**
   - Known as the "Oracle of Omaha," Buffett is the chairman and CEO of **Berkshire Hathaway** and is one of the world’s most successful investors.
   - His value investing philosophy and long-term approach to investing have made him one of the wealthiest individuals globally.

### 6. **Richard Branson (1950–Present)**
   - Founder of **Virgin Group**, Branson has expanded his business ventures into diverse sectors, including music, airlines, telecommunications, and space travel (**Virgin Galactic**).
   - His adventurous spirit and focus on customer experience have made Virgin brands iconic.

### 7. **Mark Zuckerberg (1984–Present)**
   - Co-founder and CEO of **Facebook (Meta)**, Zuckerberg revolutionized social networking and global communication.
   - His company now encompasses various social platforms (like Instagram, WhatsApp), and he is leading the charge in the development of the **Metaverse**.

### 8. **Oprah Winfrey (1954–Present)**
   - Founder of **Harpo Productions** and the **Oprah Winfrey Network (OWN)**, Winfrey is a media mogul whose influence extends across television, publishing, and digital media.
   - She built a media empire and became one of the most influential women in the world through her television show and philanthropic work.

### 9. **Larry Page (1973–Present)**
   - Co-founder of **Google**, Page has played a critical role in developing the world’s most used search engine and transforming the internet into a place where information is readily accessible.
   - Through **Alphabet Inc.**, the parent company of Google, Page has also expanded into other areas like autonomous vehicles (**Waymo**) and artificial intelligence (**DeepMind**).

### 10. **Larry Ellison (1944–Present)**
   - Co-founder of **Oracle Corporation**, Ellison transformed the software industry, particularly in the area of **database management systems**.
   - Oracle’s impact on business technology and cloud computing continues to shape how companies operate.

### 11. **Howard Schultz (1953–Present)**
   - Former CEO of **Starbucks**, Schultz turned the company into a global coffeehouse chain, reshaping the coffee culture and transforming how people consume coffee worldwide.
   - His focus on employee benefits and creating a “third place” between work and home made Starbucks an iconic brand.

### 12. **Sara Blakely (1971–Present)**
   - Founder of **Spanx**, Blakely revolutionized the fashion industry with her line of shapewear, turning it into a multi-billion-dollar company.
   - She is one of the youngest self-made female billionaires and has been a strong advocate for female entrepreneurship.

### 13. **Jack Ma (1964–Present)**
   - Founder of **Alibaba Group**, Ma built China’s largest e-commerce and tech conglomerate, shaping the global landscape of online shopping, cloud computing, and fintech.
   - His efforts have transformed how business is conducted in China and contributed to the growth of global trade.

### 14. **Sheryl Sandberg (1970–Present)**
   - Former COO of **Facebook**, Sandberg was instrumental in growing the company’s advertising business and monetizing its platform.
   - As an advocate for women in leadership, she wrote the best-selling book **Lean In** and led initiatives for women’s empowerment in the workplace.

### 15. **Evan Spiegel (1990–Present)**
   - Co-founder of **Snap Inc.**, the parent company of **Snapchat**, Spiegel revolutionized social media with ephemeral messaging and augmented reality features.
   - His company remains at the forefront of social media innovation.

### 16. **Elon Musk's Inspiration: Thomas Edison (1847–1931)**
   - Though not an entrepreneur in the modern sense, Edison was one of the greatest inventors and business founders of his time. He founded **General Electric** and holds over 1,000 patents.
   - Edison’s inventions, including the **electric light bulb** and **phonograph**, were pivotal in shaping modern industry.

### 17. **Steve Wozniak (1950–Present)**
   - Co-founder of **Apple Inc.** alongside Steve Jobs, Wozniak was the technical genius behind the early Apple computers, notably the **Apple I** and **Apple II**.
   - His engineering skills and innovation in computer hardware played a critical role in the early success of Apple.

### 18. **Ralph Lauren (1939–Present)**
   - Founder of the **Ralph Lauren Corporation**, Lauren built a global fashion empire known for its luxury clothing, accessories, and fragrances.
   - His brand’s iconic **Polo** line redefined American fashion and luxury.

### 19. **Indra Nooyi (1955–Present)**
   - Former CEO of **PepsiCo**, Nooyi led the company through a period of global expansion and diversification, including its focus on healthier products.
   - She broke barriers for women and people of color in the corporate world, being one of the top female CEOs globally.

### 20. **James Dyson (1947–Present)**
   - Founder of **Dyson Ltd.**, Dyson revolutionized the home appliance industry with his **bagless vacuum cleaner** and has expanded into other innovations like **hand dryers** and **air purifiers**.
   - His focus on engineering excellence and disruptive technology has made Dyson a globally recognized brand.

These entrepreneurs have created some of the world’s most influential companies and products, leaving a lasting impact on various industries and shaping the global economy. Their innovations have transformed how we live, work, and interact.
