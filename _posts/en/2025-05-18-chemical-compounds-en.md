Here’s a list of common chemical compounds found in households, kitchens, or everyday products, similar to sodium bicarbonate and citric acid:

1. **Sodium Bicarbonate (NaHCO₃)** - Baking soda, used in cooking, cleaning, and as an antacid.
2. **Citric Acid (C₆H₈O₇)** - Found in citrus fruits, used as a food preservative, flavoring, or cleaning agent.
3. **Acetic Acid (CH₃COOH)** - Main component of vinegar, used in cooking, cleaning, and pickling.
4. **Sodium Chloride (NaCl)** - Table salt, used for seasoning and food preservation.
5. **Sucrose (C₁₂H₂₂O₁₁)** - Table sugar, a sweetener used in food and beverages.
6. **Ethanol (C₂H₅OH)** - Alcohol found in alcoholic beverages, sanitizers, and cleaning products.
7. **Hydrogen Peroxide (H₂O₂)** - Used as a disinfectant, bleach, or wound cleaner.
8. **Ammonia (NH₃)** - Found in household cleaners, especially for glass and surfaces.
9. **Calcium Carbonate (CaCO₃)** - Found in chalk, limestone, or as an antacid (e.g., Tums).
10. **Sodium Hypochlorite (NaOCl)** - Active ingredient in bleach, used for disinfecting and whitening.
