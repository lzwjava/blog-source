### Planning a 5-Day Trip to Zhuhai, Guangdong

Zhuhai, a coastal city in Guangdong Province, China, is perfect for a relaxing 5-day getaway with its beaches, theme parks, islands, and proximity to Macau. Known as the "City of Romance" for its scenic Lovers' Road and fresh seafood, it's ideal for nature lovers, families, and culture seekers. November (your travel window) offers mild weather (around 20-25°C/68-77°F), fewer crowds, and comfortable conditions for outdoor activities—avoid summer heat or holiday peaks when prices spike.

#### Getting There
- **Flights**: Fly into Zhuhai Jinwan International Airport (ZUH) from major Chinese cities (e.g., Beijing: 3 hours) or international hubs like Hong Kong (45 minutes). Budget airlines like Spring Airlines keep costs low (~¥500-1000 one-way domestic).
- **From Nearby**: High-speed train from Guangzhou (1 hour, ¥80) or ferry/bus from Macau/Hong Kong (15-60 minutes).
- **Visa**: Most nationalities need a China visa; check for 144-hour transit visa-free if entering via Hong Kong/Macau.

#### Accommodations
Stay in Xiangzhou District for central access (e.g., near Lovers' Road). Options:
- Budget: Hostels/hotels like Zhuhai Holiday Inn Express (~¥300/night).
- Mid-range: Resorts like Chimelong Hengqin Bay Hotel (~¥600/night, family-friendly with park access).
- Luxury: InterContinental Zhuhai (~¥1000/night, sea views).
Book via Ctrip or Trip.com for deals, especially packages with ferry tickets.

#### Transportation in Zhuhai
- Metro/light rail: Efficient (e.g., Line 1 to Gongbei Port, ¥2-10/ride).
- Buses/taxis: Didi app for rides (~¥20-50 short trips); buses are cheap (~¥2).
- Ferries: For islands (~¥100 round-trip).
- Bikes: Rent via Mobike app for coastal paths (~¥5/hour).
Total transport budget: ¥200-300 for 5 days.

#### Budget Estimate (per person, excluding flights)
- Accommodations: ¥1500-3000.
- Food: ¥500-800 (seafood-focused).
- Attractions: ¥800-1200 (theme parks dominate).
- Transport: ¥200-300.
- Total: ¥3000-5300 (mid-range).

#### 5-Day Itinerary
This balanced plan mixes relaxation, adventure, and culture, drawing from popular routes. Adjust for energy levels or weather. Assume arrival Day 1 morning, departure Day 5 evening.

**Day 1: Arrival & City Introduction (Leisure & Culture)**  
Focus on acclimating with seaside vibes.  
- Morning: Arrive, check in, stroll **Lovers' Road** (Qinglü Lu)—a 28km coastal path with coconut palms, bike rentals, and views of the Zhuhai Fisher Girl Statue (iconic marble sculpture symbolizing the city's maritime history). Rent bikes for 1-2 hours.  
- Afternoon: Visit **Zhuhai Museum** (free; exhibits on local history and art) and admire the shell-shaped **Zhuhai Opera House** exterior (photo ops only).  
- Evening: Dinner at **Xijiao Market Food Street**—try fresh seafood skewers, oyster omelets, and Cantonese dim sum (~¥40).  
- **Meals**: Light breakfast on arrival; street food lunch.  
- **Distance/Transport**: Walk/metro from airport (30-45 min to center).  
- **Overnight**: Xiangzhou District hotel.

**Day 2: Theme Park Adventure (Chimelong Ocean Kingdom)**  
Dive into family fun at Asia's top ocean park.  
- Morning: Head to **Chimelong Ocean Kingdom** (¥368 entry; open 10am-7pm)—explore 8 zones with whale sharks, beluga shows, polar bears, and thrill rides like the Flying Coaster. Don't miss the Dolphin Theater and jellyfish exhibit.  
- Afternoon: Continue park exploration; optional science shows on marine conservation.  
- Evening: Dinner at on-site eateries (international/Chinese fusion, ~¥90).  
- **Meals**: Hotel breakfast; park snacks/lunch.  
- **Distance/Transport**: Metro/bus from center (40 min, ¥10).  
- **Overnight**: Same hotel or park-adjacent resort for convenience.

**Day 3: Island Escape (Dong'ao Island)**  
Nature and beaches for a chill day.  
- Morning: Ferry to **Dong'ao Island** (~¥100 round-trip from Jiuzhou Port; 40 min ride)—hike Fushan Mountain (1,008 steps to Miyue Pavilion for Macau bridge views) and relax on Nansha Bay beach (swim, collect shells).  
- Afternoon: Explore historical sites like Cannon City (Qing Dynasty fort) and stone carvings.  
- Evening: Seafood dinner from pier stalls (e.g., steamed crab at Tung Fat Restaurant, ~¥100); return ferry.  
- **Meals**: Island picnic breakfast; fresh catch lunch.  
- **Distance/Transport**: Bus to port (20 min), then ferry.  
- **Overnight**: Same hotel (or tent camp on island for ¥100 if adventurous).

**Day 4: Landmarks, Shopping & Bridge Cruise**  
Mix icons, retail therapy, and scenic views.  
- Morning: Visit **New Yuanming Palace** (¥60; recreated Summer Palace gardens with shows like Royal Festival).  
- Afternoon: Shop at **Gongbei Walking Street** (duty-free near Macau border—bargain for clothes, electronics) and **Zhuhai Jiuzhou Plaza** (modern mall).  
- Evening: **Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge Cruise** (¥150, 90 min from Wanzai Pier)—sail under the 55km engineering marvel, spotting artificial islands and city lights.  
- **Meals**: Dim sum breakfast; mall food court lunch (~¥60); cruise snacks.  
- **Distance/Transport**: Light rail to Gongbei (10 min); taxi to pier.  
- **Overnight**: Same hotel.

**Day 5: Parks, Culture & Departure**  
Wind down before heading out.  
- Morning: Relax at **Jingshan Park** (free; hilltop views, gardens) or **Hengqin Island** (Fisher Girl revisit, optional Chimelong Birds Park if time).  
- Afternoon: Quick visit to **Red Sandalwood Museum** (furniture/art exhibits, ¥50) or art museum. Lunch at a Cantonese spot (~¥70).  
- Evening: Depart via airport/train.  
- **Meals**: Hotel breakfast; farewell seafood meal.  
- **Distance/Transport**: Metro to airport (40 min).  
- **Overnight**: N/A.

#### Tips
- **Food**: Zhuhai shines with seafood—opt for fresh, local spots over tourist traps. Vegetarian options are limited but available in malls.
- **Weather/Packing**: Layer for cool evenings; bring sunscreen, hat, and reusable water bottle.
- **Crowds**: Book Chimelong tickets online; ferries sell out weekends.
- **Connectivity**: Get a SIM/eSIM (~¥100 for 10GB data); WeChat Pay/Alipay for payments.
- **Sustainability**: Use public transport; avoid single-use plastics on beaches.
- **Extensions**: Add a Macau day trip (visa-free for many via Gongbei Port).

This plan keeps things flexible and under ¥5000/person. Customize based on interests (e.g., more shopping or hiking).
