James Hamilton is a Senior Vice President and Distinguished Engineer at Amazon Web Services (AWS), where he serves on Amazon's elite S-Team (senior leadership team). He is widely regarded as one of the primary architects behind AWS's global infrastructure, overseeing the design, efficiency, reliability, and scaling of its massive network of data centers that power cloud services for millions of customers worldwide. As a key technical advisor to Amazon CEO Andy Jassy, Hamilton has been instrumental in transforming AWS from a nascent service into a $100+ billion powerhouse, focusing on innovations like custom networking hardware, power distribution, cooling systems, and proprietary silicon processors (e.g., the Graviton family).

### Career History
- **Early Days:** Before entering tech, Hamilton worked as an auto mechanic specializing in high-end exotic cars like Lamborghinis and Ferraris, honing a hands-on, problem-solving approach that he later applied to engineering challenges.
- **Microsoft (1990s–2008):** He spent nearly a decade developing SQL Server, gaining deep expertise in databases, compilers, and enterprise software. In his final role there, he served as General Manager of Microsoft Exchange Hosted Services—his introduction to cloud computing—which shaped his future direction.
- **Amazon/AWS (2009–Present):** Hamilton joined Amazon in January 2009, diving straight into AWS to tackle core infrastructure issues. Over the years, he's driven projects on data center optimization, custom ASICs (deploying over 20 million units), and EC2 innovations. He's credited with upleveling engineering standards across AWS, from storage to networking, and holds over 200 patents in database tech and data center systems.
