Here’s a clear overview of **major countries’ international dialing codes** and how their **phone number structures** usually work:

---

### China

* **Code**: `+86`
* **Structure**: Mobile numbers are 11 digits (e.g., `+86 138 1234 5678`). Landlines vary (area code 2–3 digits + 7–8 digit local number).

### United States / Canada

* **Code**: `+1` (shared under the *North American Numbering Plan, NANP*)
* **Structure**: 10 digits after `+1` → `+1 (AAA) BBB-CCCC` (area code + 7-digit number).

### United Kingdom

* **Code**: `+44`
* **Structure**: Typically 10 digits after the code, e.g., `+44 7XXX XXXXXX` for mobiles. Leading `0` is dropped in international format (`07` → `+44 7`).

### India

* **Code**: `+91`
* **Structure**: Mobile numbers are 10 digits, e.g., `+91 98765 43210`. Landline numbers have 2–4 digit area codes + local number.

### Hong Kong

* **Code**: `+852`
* **Structure**: 8 digits, no area codes (e.g., `+852 5123 4567`).

### Macau

* **Code**: `+853`
* **Structure**: 8 digits, no area codes (e.g., `+853 6612 3456`).

### Japan

* **Code**: `+81`
* **Structure**: Drop leading 0 → mobiles look like `+81 90-1234-5678`.

### South Korea

* **Code**: `+82`
* **Structure**: Drop leading 0 → mobiles look like `+82 10-1234-5678`.

### Singapore

* **Code**: `+65`
* **Structure**: 8 digits, no area codes (e.g., `+65 8123 4567`).

### Australia

* **Code**: `+61`
* **Structure**: Drop leading 0 → mobiles look like `+61 4XX XXX XXX`.

### Germany

* **Code**: `+49`
* **Structure**: Drop leading 0 → mobiles like `+49 151 12345678`. Area codes vary for landlines.

### France

* **Code**: `+33`
* **Structure**: Drop leading 0 → mobiles like `+33 6 12 34 56 78`.

### Italy

* **Code**: `+39`
* **Structure**: Mobile numbers often 10 digits, starting with `3`.

### Brazil

* **Code**: `+55`
* **Structure**: `(AA) NNNNN-NNNN` (2-digit area code + 8–9 digit number).

### Russia

* **Code**: `+7`
* **Structure**: 10 digits after code → `+7 (AAA) BBB-CC-DD`.
