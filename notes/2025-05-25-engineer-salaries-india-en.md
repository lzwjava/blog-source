Software engineer salaries in India’s major tech hubs like Hyderabad and Pune vary significantly based on experience, company type, and specific roles. Below is a breakdown for beginner (0–2 years), mid-level (5 years), and senior (10 years) software engineers, focusing on these cities, based on recent data. Salaries are in Indian Rupees (INR) per annum, and ranges account for variations across companies (e.g., MNCs, startups, product-based vs. service-based firms) and specializations (e.g., AI, full stack, cloud computing). Note that these are approximate averages and can vary based on skills, company reputation, and negotiation.

### **Beginner Software Engineer (0–2 Years Experience)**
- **Hyderabad**:
  - **Salary Range**: ₹4–8 lakhs per annum (LPA)
  - **Monthly Estimate**: ₹33,000–₹66,000
  - **Details**: Hyderabad, a major tech hub with companies like Microsoft, Google, and TCS, offers competitive entry-level salaries. Fresh graduates from top-tier colleges (e.g., IITs, NITs) or those with skills in high-demand areas like AI or cloud computing may hit the higher end (₹7–8 LPA). Startups may offer slightly lower base pay but include equity options.[](https://www.weekday.works/post/average-salary-software-engineer-india)[](https://www.upgrad.com/blog/software-engineer-developer-salary-in-india-freshers-experienced/)
- **Pune**:
  - **Salary Range**: ₹4–7.5 LPA
  - **Monthly Estimate**: ₹33,000–₹62,500
  - **Details**: Pune, home to firms like Infosys, Wipro, and product-based companies, has slightly lower entry-level salaries than Hyderabad but remains competitive. The range depends on the company size and specialization, with product-based firms paying closer to ₹6–7.5 LPA.[](https://www.weekday.works/post/average-salary-software-engineer-india)[](https://www.glassdoor.co.in/Salaries/pune-software-engineer-salary-SRCH_IL.0%2C4_IM1072_KO5%2C22.htm)

### **Key Factors Influencing Salaries**
1. **Location**: Hyderabad tends to offer slightly higher salaries than Pune due to its status as a major tech hub with a higher concentration of global tech giants. Both cities pay more than tier-2 cities due to higher living costs and demand for talent.[](https://www.weekday.works/post/average-salary-software-engineer-india)
2. **Company Type**: Product-based companies (e.g., Google, Microsoft) pay 20–30% more than service-based firms (e.g., TCS, Infosys). Startups may offer equity to offset lower base salaries.[](https://www.weekday.works/post/average-salary-software-engineer-india)[](https://www.crampete.com/blogs/software-developer-salary-in-india/)
3. **Specialization**: Skills in AI, machine learning, cloud computing (AWS, Azure), or DevOps can increase salaries by 10–20% across all experience levels.[](https://www.weekday.works/post/average-salary-software-engineer-india)[](https://www.upgrad.com/blog/software-engineer-developer-salary-in-india-freshers-experienced/)
4. **Education**: Graduates from IITs, NITs, or other top-tier institutions often secure higher starting salaries (₹1–2 LPA more).[](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/software-engineer-salary/)
5. **Additional Compensation**: Bonuses, stock options, and benefits can add ₹50,000–₹5,00,000 annually, especially in MNCs.[](https://www.levels.fyi/t/software-engineer/locations/india)
