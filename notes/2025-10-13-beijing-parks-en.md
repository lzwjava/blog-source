Beijing is home to numerous parks, ranging from historic imperial gardens to modern green spaces. Based on popular recommendations, here are some of the top parks to visit, including highlights for each:

- **Temple of Heaven**: A UNESCO World Heritage site with stunning architecture, ancient altars, and vast grounds perfect for morning exercises.
- **Jingshan Park**: Offers panoramic views of the Forbidden City from its central hill; great for photos and relaxed strolls.
- **Beihai Park**: Features a large lake, pavilions, and lotus blooms in summer; ideal for boating and exploring imperial history.
- **Old Summer Palace (Yuanmingyuan)**: Ruins of a grand Qing dynasty estate with gardens, lakes, and European-style architecture remnants.
- **Fragrant Hills Park**: Famous for autumn foliage and hiking trails up to a temple; cable car access available.
- **The Imperial Garden of The Palace Museum**: A serene escape within the Forbidden City, with ancient trees and pavilions.
- **Yuyuantan Park**: Known for cherry blossoms in spring and a central lake; popular for picnics and boating.
- **Beijing Botanical Garden**: Extensive plant collections, including roses, peonies, and a tropical greenhouse; near the Ming Tombs.
- **Ritan Park**: A quiet spot with temples, lawns, and tai chi sessions; good for locals' vibe.
- **Zhongshan Park**: Adjacent to the Forbidden City, with traditional gardens, peonies, and a revolutionary history site.
- **Chaoyang Park**: Modern park with lakes, amusement rides, and seasonal flower displays; family-friendly.
- **Olympic Forest Park**: Built for the 2008 Games, featuring wetlands, sculptures, and cycling paths.
- **Zizhuyuan Park (Black Bamboo Park)**: Peaceful with bamboo groves, ponds, and traditional pavilions for walking or jogging.
- **Ditan Park (Temple of Earth)**: Imperial altar site with ancient trees and open spaces for kite-flying.
- **Longtan Lake Park**: Scenic lakeside paths, bridges, and gardens; lively with locals dancing and exercising.
