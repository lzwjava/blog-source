**Chapter 6: Administrative Law**

* **What is Administrative Law?**
    * Defines the powers and procedures of government administrative agencies.
    * Governs the relationship between administrative bodies and individuals/organizations.
    * Ensures administrative actions are lawful, reasonable, and fair.
* **Principles of Administrative Law:**
    * **Legality:** Administrative actions must have a legal basis.
    * **Reasonableness:** Decisions should be logical and proportionate.
    * **Fairness (Procedural Due Process):** Individuals affected by administrative decisions have the right to be heard and receive fair procedures.
    * **Accountability:** Administrative bodies are accountable for their actions.
* **Administrative Agencies:**
    * Different levels and types of government bodies with specific administrative functions (e.g., environmental protection, taxation, public health).
    * Sources of their powers (statutes, regulations).
* **Powers of Administrative Agencies:**
    * Rule-making (issuing regulations).
    * Adjudication (making decisions in specific cases).
    * Investigation and enforcement.
    * Licensing and permitting.
* **Judicial Review:**
    * The process by which courts review the legality and validity of administrative actions.
    * Grounds for judicial review (e.g., ultra vires, procedural impropriety, unreasonableness).

**Chapter 7: Criminal Law**

* **What is Criminal Law?**
    * Deals with conduct that is considered harmful to society as a whole.
    * Defines crimes and prescribes punishments.
    * Distinguished from civil law (which deals with private disputes).
* **Elements of a Crime:**
    * **Actus Reus:** The guilty act (the physical act of committing the crime).
    * **Mens Rea:** The guilty mind (the mental state of the accused at the time of the act).
    * **Causation:** A link between the act and the resulting harm.
    * (Sometimes) **Concurrence:** The actus reus and mens rea must occur at the same time.
* **Types of Criminal Offenses:**
    * Felonies (more serious crimes with harsher penalties).
    * Misdemeanors (less serious crimes with lighter penalties).
    * Examples of different categories of offenses (e.g., property crimes, violent crimes, white-collar crimes).
* **Criminal Procedure:**
    * The steps involved in investigating, prosecuting, and adjudicating criminal cases.
    * Key stages: investigation, arrest, detention, indictment, trial, sentencing, appeal.
    * Rights of the accused (e.g., right to counsel, right to a fair trial).
* **China’s Criminal Law:**
    * Focus on the *Criminal Law of the People's Republic of China*.
    * Specific offenses and their definitions under Chinese law.
    * The role of the procuratorate and the courts in the criminal justice system.
    * Emphasis on social harmony and public order.

**Chapter 8: Civil Law**

* **What is Civil Law?**
    * Governs private relationships between individuals and organizations.
    * Deals with disputes over rights and obligations.
    * Aims to provide remedies for harm caused.
* **General Principles of Civil Law:**
    * Autonomy of will (freedom to enter into contracts).
    * Good faith.
    * Fairness.
    * Liability for wrongful acts.
* **Contract Law:**
    * Formation of contracts (offer, acceptance, consideration).
    * Terms and conditions of contracts.
    * Breach of contract and remedies (e.g., damages, specific performance).
* **Tort Law (Law of Delicts):**
    * Deals with civil wrongs that cause harm to others.
    * Types of torts (e.g., negligence, intentional torts, strict liability).
    * Remedies for torts (primarily monetary damages).
* **Property Law:**
    * Deals with rights and interests in tangible and intangible property.
    * Ownership, possession, and other property rights.
    * Transfer of property.
* **China’s Civil Law:**
    * Focus on the *Civil Code of the People's Republic of China*.
    * Comprehensive legislation covering various aspects of civil relationships.
    * Emphasis on protecting individual rights and promoting social and economic order.

* **Principles of International Law:**
