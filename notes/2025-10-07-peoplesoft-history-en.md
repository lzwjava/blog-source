### Comprehensive Introduction to PeopleSoft

#### History and Evolution
PeopleSoft originated as a company founded in 1987 by Dave Duffield and Ken Morris in California, initially focusing on human resources (HR) software to address the limitations of mainframe-based systems at the time. The company quickly expanded its offerings into a full suite of enterprise applications, emphasizing user-friendly interfaces and relational database technology. By the early 2000s, PeopleSoft had become a leader in enterprise resource planning (ERP) software, competing with giants like SAP and Oracle.

In 2003, Oracle launched a hostile takeover bid for PeopleSoft, which culminated in a successful acquisition in January 2005 for $10.3 billion. This merger integrated PeopleSoft into Oracle's portfolio, rebranding it as Oracle PeopleSoft Applications. Post-acquisition, Oracle committed to maintaining and enhancing the platform, introducing innovations like Fluid User Interface (UI) and cloud deployment options. As of 2025, PeopleSoft has over 30 years of legacy, serving thousands of organizations worldwide, particularly in higher education, government, healthcare, and manufacturing sectors. Oracle has reaffirmed its long-term support, extending premier support through at least 2036 under a rolling 10-year model announced in March 2025.

#### Overview
PeopleSoft is a comprehensive suite of cloud-enabled ERP applications designed for mid-to-large enterprises with complex operational needs. It provides integrated solutions across core business functions, leveraging a metadata-driven architecture for flexibility and scalability. At its core is PeopleTools, Oracle's proprietary development platform that powers the applications' runtime, customization, and integration capabilities. This foundation allows organizations to adapt the software without extensive coding, supporting both on-premises and cloud deployments (including Oracle Cloud Infrastructure, or OCI).

Unlike more rigid ERP systems, PeopleSoft emphasizes configurability, enabling businesses to tailor workflows, reports, and interfaces to their specific requirements. It supports multi-language, multi-currency operations and integrates seamlessly with other Oracle products, such as Fusion Cloud Applications, for hybrid environments.

#### Key Modules and Functionality
PeopleSoft's modular design covers a wide range of business areas, allowing selective implementation. The primary pillars include:

- **Human Capital Management (HCM)**: A flagship module handling the full employee lifecycle, from recruiting and onboarding to performance management, payroll, benefits administration, and talent development. It includes tools for workforce planning, learning management, and absence tracking. HCM is particularly strong in higher education (e.g., student information systems) and government compliance.

- **Financial Management (Financials)**: Encompasses general ledger, accounts payable/receivable, asset management, billing, expenses, and project costing. It supports advanced financial reporting, budgeting, and grant management, with robust audit trails for regulatory adherence (e.g., SOX compliance).

- **Supply Chain Management (SCM)**: Covers procurement, inventory, order management, manufacturing, and supplier portals. Features like the Comprehensive Supplier Portal enable self-service vendor interactions, while inventory optimization tools reduce costs and improve efficiency.

- **Customer Relationship Management (CRM)**: Focuses on sales, marketing, service, and field service automation, with analytics for customer segmentation and campaign management.

- **Campus Solutions (for Education)**: Tailored for universities and colleges, managing admissions, student records, financial aid, and alumni engagement.

- **Other Specialized Modules**: Include Real Estate Management, Enterprise Learning Management (ELM), and Industry-specific solutions like Public Sector or Healthcare.

These modules are interconnected via a common data model, ensuring real-time visibility and process automation across the enterprise.
