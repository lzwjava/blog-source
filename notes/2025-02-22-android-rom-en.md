If you're looking for an open-source and popular Android ROM, here are some top recommendations based on their widespread use, community support, and active development as of February 22, 2025:

1. **LineageOS**  
   - **Why it's recommended**: LineageOS is arguably the most popular open-source Android ROM, succeeding the well-known CyanogenMod. It’s highly regarded for its stability, broad device support, and close-to-stock Android experience with added customization options. It’s maintained by a large community, ensuring regular updates and compatibility with a wide range of devices, from newer models to older ones no longer supported by manufacturers.
   - **Key Features**: Minimal bloatware, extensive device support, monthly security updates, and a focus on user personalization.
   - **Open Source**: Fully open-source, with its code available on GitHub.
   - **Popularity**: It’s a go-to choice for enthusiasts due to its reliability and vast user base.

2. **GrapheneOS**  
   - **Why it's recommended**: GrapheneOS is a privacy- and security-focused ROM that’s gaining traction among users prioritizing data protection. While it’s more niche than LineageOS, its popularity is growing, especially among those looking to avoid Google’s ecosystem. It’s based on AOSP (Android Open Source Project) and is actively developed with a strong emphasis on hardening the OS against exploits.
   - **Key Features**: Enhanced privacy features, no Google services by default (though compatible with sandboxed Google Play if needed), and regular security updates.
   - **Open Source**: Completely open-source, with transparent development on GitHub.
   - **Popularity**: Limited to Pixel devices, but highly respected in privacy-conscious circles.

3. **/e/OS**  
   - **Why it's recommended**: /e/OS is a fork of LineageOS designed to be a Google-free alternative, replacing Google services with open-source equivalents like microG. It’s user-friendly and appeals to those seeking a balance between privacy and usability. Its popularity has increased with pre-installed options on devices from the eFoundation.
   - **Key Features**: De-Googled experience, pre-installed privacy-focused apps (e.g., Signal), and a clean interface.
   - **Open Source**: Fully open-source, built on LineageOS foundations.
   - **Popularity**: Growing steadily, especially in Europe, with a dedicated community.

4. **Paranoid Android**  
   - **Why it's recommended**: Paranoid Android is a well-known AOSP-based ROM that blends performance with innovative UI features. It’s popular among users who want a polished experience with unique customizations not found in stock Android. It has a loyal following and consistent updates.
   - **Key Features**: Features like Hover notifications, Pie controls, and Dynamic System Bars, alongside a smooth user experience.
   - **Open Source**: Open-source with code available for review.
   - **Popularity**: A favorite among custom ROM enthusiasts for its balance of style and functionality.

### Recommendation
- **Best Overall**: **LineageOS** is the top pick if you want a widely supported, open-source ROM with a strong community and broad device compatibility.
- **For Privacy**: **GrapheneOS** if you own a Pixel device and prioritize security over customization.
- **Google-Free Option**: **/e/OS** for a practical de-Googled experience with growing popularity.

Each of these ROMs is open-source, actively maintained, and enjoys significant popularity in the Android custom ROM scene. Your choice depends on your device compatibility and whether you prioritize customization, privacy, or ease of use. Check the official websites (e.g., lineageos.org, grapheneos.org, e.foundation) for supported devices and installation guides.
