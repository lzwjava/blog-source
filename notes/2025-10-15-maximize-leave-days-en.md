### Maximizing Your 5 Annual Leave Days with Chinese Public Holidays

As a contractor in China, you're smart to think about "tiao xiu" (holiday adjustments) and chaining your limited annual leave with public holidays. This can turn short breaks into full family vacations without burning all your days at once. The key is to focus on upcoming 3-5 day holidays where adding 2-3 leave days creates a 5-7 day block—perfect for domestic travel with wife and kids (easy flights, no visas, kid-friendly spots). Save your remaining days for one longer extension if needed.

**Tips for Arranging Leave:**
- **Book early:** Submit leave requests 1-2 months ahead, especially for bank/contractor roles where approvals can lag. Check your contract for rules on holiday combos.
- **Prioritize 3-day holidays:** Use 2 leave days to "sandwich" them (e.g., 1 before + 1 after) for a seamless 5-day trip. This leaves 3 days for another short break.
- **Family focus:** Aim for 4-7 days total to avoid kid fatigue. Fly mid-week to cut costs/crowds. Use high-speed trains for domestic spots under 5 hours from major cities (e.g., Shanghai/Beijing).
- **Split your 5 days:** Do two 5-day trips (2 + 3 days leave) or one 7-day (3 days) + two 3-day weekends (1 day each). Avoid golden weeks like Spring/National if you hate crowds—opt for shoulder seasons like April/May.
- **Practical:** Factor in school holidays for kids (align with these). Budget for family: Domestic flights ~¥500-1500/person round-trip; hotels ¥800-1500/night for family rooms.

### Recommended Family-Friendly Destinations
For 5-7 day trips, stick to domestic spots—safe, affordable, and packed with kid-pleasing activities like animals, history, and nature. Assume you're in a big city like Shanghai or Beijing; adjust flights accordingly. Here's a curated list based on popular family picks:

1. **Chengdu (Sichuan) – Pandas & Chill Vibes (Ideal for Dragon Boat or Qingming, 5 days)**
   - Why family? World-famous panda bases (Chengdu Research Base—kids love feeding/holding them), plus hotpot food adventures and easy day trips to Leshan Giant Buddha.
   - Itinerary: Day 1-2: Pandas + city zoo. Day 3: Train to Leshan (2h). Day 4-5: Relax in teahouses or Du Fu Thatched Cottage park.
   - Travel: 2-3h flight from most cities. Cost: ¥3000-5000/family.
   - Best for: Younger kids; mild weather in spring/summer.

2. **Xi'an (Shaanxi) – History with Fun Twists (Great for Labor Day, 5-6 days)**
   - Why family? Terracotta Warriors (like a real-life Lego set), ancient city walls for biking, and Muslim Quarter street food (kid-friendly skewers/ice cream).
   - Itinerary: Day 1: Arrival + city wall bike ride. Day 2-3: Warriors + Huaqing Hot Springs (swim in pools). Day 4-5: Tang Dynasty show + free time.
   - Travel: 2h flight or high-speed train. Cost: ¥4000-6000/family.
   - Best for: School-age kids into stories; cooler in May.

3. **Hainan (Sanya Beaches) – Beach Escape (For a relaxing Qingming extension, 5 days)**
   - Why family? White-sand beaches, water parks (Atlantis Sanya—slides, aquariums), and resort pools. No history overload—just sun and sea.
   - Itinerary: Day 1-2: Beach/resort hop. Day 3: Theme park or snorkeling. Day 4-5: Yanoda Rainforest light hike + spa for parents.
   - Travel: 3h flight. Cost: ¥5000-8000/family (beach season starts April).
   - Best for: All ages; escape city heat, but book AC hotels.

4. **Guilin/Yangshuo (Guangxi) – Nature Magic (Bonus for longer Labor Day, 6 days)**
   - Why family? Karst mountains, Li River bamboo rafting (gentle, scenic), and cave explorations (Silver Cave—cool lights/effects for kids).
   - Itinerary: Day 1-2: Guilin Reed Flute Cave + river cruise. Day 3-5: Yangshuo biking/impression show + West Street markets.
   - Travel: 2h flight to Guilin. Cost: ¥3500-5500/family.
   - Best for: Outdoor-loving families; stunning photos.

Start with one of these based on your kids' ages/interests—Chengdu for animal lovers, Hainan for downtime. If international appeals (e.g., Singapore Zoo for a 7-day splurge), use 3-4 leave days on a 3-day holiday, but factor visas/flights. Track next year's holidays via official sites for planning.
