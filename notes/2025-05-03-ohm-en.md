### Ohm’s Law and Major Contributions
In the 1820s, Ohm turned his attention to the study of electric currents, inspired by the recent advancements in electrodynamics. The law states that the current (I) flowing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage (V) across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance (R) of the conductor.
