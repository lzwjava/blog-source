Hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) has a faint, sharp, and slightly pungent smell due to its chemical properties and interactions. As a reactive oxygen species, it can release small amounts of oxygen gas (O₂) when it decomposes, especially in the presence of light, heat, or impurities. This decomposition can produce a subtle odor, often described as slightly acrid or bleach-like, because of the interaction of released oxygen with organic compounds or moisture in the air. The smell is more noticeable in concentrated solutions (e.g., 30% or higher), as dilute solutions (like 3% used in households) are often odorless or nearly so. Additionally, trace impurities or stabilizers in commercial hydrogen peroxide can contribute to the perceived smell.
